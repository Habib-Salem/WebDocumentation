Background

The Topic

The foundation of the website proposal will be based upon the not-for-profit Charity "Australian Red Cross Lifeblood". The ARCL plays an important role in the healthcare system by ensuring that patients in Australian hospitals receive life-saving blood products, delivering world-class research and provide expertise in diagnostic, transplantation and other clinical services, so helping reduce suffering (ACNC, n.d). Particularly, the intended call-to-action of the Australian Red Cross Lifeblood is to encourage eligible users to donate blood in order to achieve their ultimate goal of saving lives.

The Australian Red Cross Blood Service collects around 1.3 million blood donations anually of which is used to help people with medical conditions that require blood for suitable treatment. For instance, 34% of donated red blood cells are used to help treat people with cancer and blood diseases such as haemophilia (Better Health, n.d). Correspondingly, medical researchers use donated blood in order to develop and test new treatments for various medical conditions such as blod clots, heart attack, stroke and cancer.

The Implications of the Current Website and Process

While an existing website exists to facillitate this process of blood donation, the user experience and process is deeply flawed in its ability to effectively guide the user to achieve its intended call-to-action of donating blood. This is particularly shown through two major failiures of the website including:

  • A clustered and ambigious user interface and experience: The current information architecture is scattered among the website; making it excessively difficult for the user to navigate their way through an intended easy process of donating blood. Correspondingly, the oversaturation of visual CSS elements on various pages of the website make it overwhelming for the user to navigate their way through the already ambigious information architecture.
  • The disposal and refusal of un-eligible users who cannot donate blood: The inherent flaw behind the sheer concept of the Australian Red Cross Lifeblood is that it neglects users who are not eligible for blood. Users who have already taken a considerable amount of time to make their way through a convoluted process of attempting to donate blood should not be abruptly turned away from the process but rather provided with an alternative means to support the intent of the cause. For instance, raising awareness and promoting the Australian Red Cross Lifeblood through social media.
Hence, the intended website design proposal will capatalise on these fundamental flaws that the current website presents. This will be achieved by creating and designing a website with an effective user experience and interface that will facillitate and guide both eligible and non-eligible users to navigate through an effective process to achieve the intended goals of the Australian Red Cross Lifeblood.

Research

Both primary and secondary research was conducted in order to provide context of the function and concept of the intended web design proposal.

Primary Research

Primary research had been conducted through the form of informal interviews. Through this, three interviewees from various backgrounds were asked on their opinions on the current Australian Red Cross Lifeblood website and had used methods such as the think-aloud protocol to justify their thought processess while exploring the website. Correspondingly, interviewees were asked contextual information regarding to how they commit to various charities as well as their experiences with blood donation. Thus, it was through this primary research in the form of informal interviews that had expanded the designers understanding and empathy regarding the interaction between people and charities; particularly blood donation.

Here are some key quotes regarding the interviewees opinons in the current Red Cross Lifeblood website as brought about by the think-aloud protocol.

"The website is kind of clustered and overwhelming to use. I honestly don't know where to go first since everything is just popping out at me" Jessica Tan, 39
"I really like the colour scheme of the website; specifically the red colour that represents blood of which is what this website is all about. However, this website needs a bit of a cleanup because there are too many buttons and options to click on" Nathan Trung, 36
"I was actually going to consider giving blood, so I spent all my time during this long and tedious process just to be told I was not eligible. I don't know why I had bothered to put so much effort in the first place" Emily Smith, 17

Moreover, here are some additional key quotes regarding the interviewees experience with charities in general as well as blood donation:

"I have been donating blood for nearly 6 years now. It is an effective way for me to support others in need while I don't have to spend too much" Jessica Tan, 39
"I always donate clothes to St Vincent De Paul society, however I have slightly considered blood donation but never really encourage myself to go enough. To be very honest, I don't even know if I am eligible or not" Nathan Trung, 36
"In terms of charity work, I have participated in many charities and fundraisers at school, however I have never donated blood because I don't think I can until I turn 18. If there was any other possible way I could support the cause, I definitely would" Emily Smith, 17

Secondary Research

Secondary research had been conducted in order to find various statistics on how blood donations play a vital role in saving lives of which the Australian Red Cross Lifeblood facillitates. These statistics would serve to play a vital role in emphasising the sheer impact and influence that blood donation has on society. Hence, these include:

  • One in three Australians will need blood or blood products in their lifetime (ARCL, 2019)
  • One in 30 Australians give blood each year (3%) (ARCL, 2019)
  • One blood donation can save up to three lives (ARCL, 2019)
  • Australia needs more than 31,000 donations every week (ARCL, 2019)
Correspondingly, donor statistics between 2018 - 2019 included:
  • 523,688 Australians voluntarily gave blood, plasma or platelets to help others (ACRL, 2019)
  • 978,196 blood products delivered to hospitals and healthcare providers to help Australian patients (ACRL, 2019)
Therefore, the intended web proposal will effectively serve to provide an effective user experience and interface in order to successfully facillitate the donation of blood. Correspondingly, the design proposal will also introduce two new call-to-actions in order to accommodate for those users who are ineligible and unable to donate blood. Hence, this includes:
  • Allowing the user to raise awareness through social media: For instance, clicking on a pre-filled facebook post that users can post to their social media wall for their friends and family to see.
  • Allowing the user to donate money: For instance, the user can choose to donate a specified amount of money to the Australian Red Cross Lifeblood. These donations can go towards costs such as advertising, wages and various expenses of the business.
